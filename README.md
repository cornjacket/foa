# Youth Team Basketball Practice Plan

## Philosophy

**Time training, not time watching.**

- Vary exercises to keep kids interested.
- Keep kids moving — no long lines. Drills should minimize standing/waiting so kids don't get bored or distracted.
- **Split lines:** instead of one big line of 9, run **3 lines of 3**. Fewer kids talking, less goofing off, looks better.

## Dribbling Routines

Routines are run **down and back**. Full list of routines in our rotation:

- Between the legs
- Under drags
- Cross jabs
- Strongside spins
- Weakside spins
- Hips to drop step
- Speed stops

**Selection rule:** Pick **4 routines** for each practice. Keep the same 4 across several practices until athletes are competent at all of them.

**Sliding window:** Once *every* athlete can perform a routine correctly, that routine drops out and a new routine slides in to take its place.

## Drive-N-* Drills

Each practice, take **one of the 4 dribbling routines** and apply it inside a finishing drill:

- Drive-n-layup
- Drive-n-high-off-the-glass

Notes:

- Sometimes substitute a move we are *not* currently working in the dribbling section (e.g., pro hop) to keep the move bank fresh.
- Always run with **both left and right hands**.

## Defensive Drills

Emphasize defense in **every** practice. Mix individual and team drills.

- **3-man weave → immediate 2v1**
- **2v1 + 1 trailer** — offense picks which basket to attack; defense must communicate.
- **1v1 half court** — rebounder becomes offense, wing-boxout athlete becomes defense; immediate containment + defense.
- **5-man shell drill — PAC defense** (in-the-gap help defense).
  - *Variant — pass-and-react:* offense only passes the ball (no driving/scoring); defense reacts at full speed to every pass. Builds rotation reps and communication.
  - *Variant — paint-only scoring:* offense can only score in the paint — forces defense to collapse and rotate, and rewards interior help.
- **4v3 team defense** — offense (4) passes around to the open man; defense (3) reacts and covers based on ball movement.

## Shooting Drills

Use our offensive sets as the framework for shooting and layup work.

- **3-man zoom → elbow jump shot**
- **3-man zoom → drive → kick-out to corner → swing to wing for the 3**
- **Iverson → outlet pass to shooter for the 3**

## Fun Stuff

- **3 Card Monty** — passing drill. Offense (3) vs. Defense (2) in zone coverage. Offense can only score from behind the extended free throw line.
- **2v1** — sideline-to-sideline, or full court.

## Cardio Drills

Pick one or more per practice.

- **Half-court free throw miss → sprints**

## Reviewing Offense

- **5-O** — Downstream + Downstream + Zoom
- **5-O Pick 'n Roll** — Drive, Pass to roll, Elbow jumper, Pop
- **Motion** — off of 5-O
- **Regular** (used against 2-3) — attack vectors: 45, high post, short corner, overload one side
- **21** — off of Regular *(confirm)*
- **Dive** — off of Regular *(confirm)*
- **4-O** (possibly called "Chins" — research) — currently used to set up Iverson
